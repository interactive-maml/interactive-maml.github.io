{`Local curvature in action`}
{#if show_b} {/if} -->
Here you can play around with a simple function space and see how it transforms when you tweak the Hessian. The Hessian of \(f\) is displayed to the bottom right. It is symmetric and hence has three free parameters \(a, b\) and \(c\). In addition to the function space, we also display the direction of the gradient \( \nabla f(x) = C (x - \frac{1}{2}) + g\), as well as the first-order approximation to the gradient, i.e., \(g\). Setting \(c = 0\) and moving either \(a\) or \(b\) nicely demonstrates the "curving" the Hessians does to the space. How accurate is the first-order approximation for different Hessians? What happens to the space if one of the parameters is negative? Can you determine how each parameter is contributing to the space transform?
